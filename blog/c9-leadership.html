Leadership

What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?
Managers are made, Leaders are born

I've had the fortune of working with a number of managers, some of whom have been great leaders. What separates them is their compassion. Compassion for their work, staff and what they want to leave behind.

One of the departments I worked in had a change over, including a new manager, whom I've never met as I was in hospital when he started working. I received an email from him, asking me how I was doing after the operation, and whether it was appropriate to visit me. I thought to myself 'Wow! We've never met but he genuinely cares about me, even though I may never be able to go back to work.' He didn't have to do any of that. He could have just focussed on the members of staff who were 'fully operational'.

On another occasion, in another lifetime, in another department, I had to take time off work, and sent an email to my manager that I'd be at least a week off my duties. The reply was short and sweet: 'Do you have a medical certificate?' That was it, no how are you or hope you feel better soon, but straight to the practicalitiies. I suppose he had to file the documents in a safe place.

So, what is the moral of the story? Is there one? I'm learning to look and cnsider all perspectives as part of my mindfulness training. But I really can't figure out the reply I received, nor explain the reason for it. I've tried really hard.Let me move on...

Have a difficult converstaion. How did it go?

I'd like to start by saying that one never looks forward to having a difficult conversation. I was avoiding this conversation but finally took the plunge. Also took some deep breaths. I tried, really hard, to see the situation from the other person's perspective. I accepted that he was a human, with needs and wants, and limitations. I listened first and foremost. I acknowledged what he said, and showed respect. It was genuine, and I think he could see that. So when it was my turn, he also listened. It was a fruitful conversation.
